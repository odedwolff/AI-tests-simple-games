
please create following simple html game:

§1.1 OVERVIEW:
a simple 2d game. 

§1.2 DEFINITION:
d-pixel: D_PIXEL_SIZE = 1/1000 of the whole screen width

§1.3
GAME WORLD:
a single screen is the entire world of the game. camera is static, nothing exists outside of the screen. 

§1.3.1 physics:
gravity is G= 123 d-pixels/second square


§1.4 GAME OBJECTS:
§1.4.1 two peddles 
 2 paddles moving in a 2D space:
each paddle is a rectangle of length AVT_LENGTH = 75 d-pixel and thickness of AVT_THICK = 10 d-pixel. at any given time, the active paddle is brighter to indicate that. 
each paddle has a front and a back. the front is one of the rectenagle's long edges, and the back is the edge against it. 
each paddle has a small bump stikcing out of the the middel of its back. it is about 5 d-pixel deep.

§1.4.1.1 contorl:
each paddle can move around the 2D space using the arrows keys. it can rotate in either direction with the Q and W keys. 
2 paddles are controlled by the same keys - at any given time there is one active paddle. the active paddle can be switched using the TAB key.
§1.4.1.2 load back and release:
when pressing SPACE KEY	the back loading begins: the active peddle withdraws back, perpendicular to its long axis, in the direction the peddele's back is directed.  
when releasing SPACE KEY, the withdrown peddle moves back to is pre load position. load back speed is LOADBACK_SPD = 60 d-pixel/sec, max load back range is LOADBACK_RANGE=75 d-pixel, relase speed is MAX_REL_SPPED = 250 d-pixel/sec if the full backload range was reached, or proportionally slower of less was reached

§1.4.1.2 physics:
paddles are not affected by gravity. 


§1.4.2 the ball 
1 small ball with radius BALL_RAD = 5 d-pixel. 
the ball is affected by gravity. when a ball is hitting a paddle it is bounces back according to normal physics, considering it also the energy that the peddle might bring into the collision  

 


§1.5
UI 
the game panel is at the center of the screen. there is a thin green frame to border it from all directions. there is a 150 d-pixel whide margin on either side, and 10 d-pixel size margin above and bellow the game panel. on the left panel there is a devloper panel. it contains controls for the following constants: G, INIT_SPEED, INIT_BALL_ANGL, BALL_RAD. make sure these controls are not maintaining focus after setting a value, to prevent the values changed accidently while playing game.  
on the right panel there are control buttons like restart and pause 

color scheme:
dominant dark - gray, with some green, yellow and orange. each paddle has 2 tones, one for each of it's long side face, or any other way to help visualy distinguish between 2 long face of the paddle

§1.6
GAME PLAY:
§1.6.1 
start:
the 2 paddles are on both sides of the game panel, facing each other, their backs to the left and rigtht frame walls respectevely. 
each paddle's long axis (the 75 d-px AVT_WIDTH dimension) is aligned with the Y-axis, i.e. pointing top-to-bottom. The short axis (AVT_THICK = 10 d-px) is horizontal.
the ball shot from the bottom left corner of the game panel in INIT_BALL_ANGL = 49 and at speed INIT_BALL_SPEED = 400 d-pixel / sec. 
§1.6.2 
restart conditions:
each time the ball goes out of the screen bounds (that is, hitting top, bottom, left or right limits - there are no wall or cieling around) there is a short countdown and the game restarts as per above
